Wellcome to the World of Coffee

Coffee is a plant

Coffee plant belongs to a genus of plants known as Coffea. It is estimated that there are anywhere from 25 to 100 species of coffee plants. Ever since there are disagreements on the exact classification of the coffee, since coffee plants can range widely. In the commercial coffee industry, there are two species - Arabica, C.Arabica and Robusta, Canephora. It is believed that Arabica comes from South Sudan and Ethiopia, while Robusta comes from West Africa. Through centuries, colonization was responsible for expanding the use of coffee. Today, coffee is maybe the most common drink worldwide regardless of cultures.



Brands

There are thousands of coffee brands, it depends on the market and trends in the specific areas. Quality and flavour are the elements that impact the trends and demands on the market. Everything from the variety of the plant, the chemistry of the soil, the weather, the amount of rainfall and sunshine, and even the precise altitude at which the coffee grows can affect the taste of the final product. The key variables to search for when deciding on quality are the distinctions between coffees from countries, growing regions and plantations worldwide. Every coffee as final product need to have its origin stated on the packaging.

Coffee from around the World

Africa

The Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, Uganda, Malawi as grand manufacturers of the coffee brands and coffee products. In Africa both Arabica and Robusta have their places on plantations and in processes of production. They are well-known and well-liked worldwide. An Ethiopian coffee tends to offer a remarkable and bold statement: full flavored, a bit down-to-earth and full bodied. It is aslo the case with other countries of coffee origins in Africa. Interesting fact is that the Ivory Coast stands for one of the world's largest producers of Robusta coffee, which is strongly aromatic with a light body and low acidity.
